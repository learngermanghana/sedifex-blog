Inventory mistakes often look small day to day but become expensive over time.

## 10 common mistakes

1. Selling items not posted into the system.
2. Receiving stock without quantity verification.
3. Mixing personal and business stock usage.
4. No cycle counts between major stock audits.
5. Ignoring dead stock reports.
6. No expiry monitoring for sensitive items.
7. Weak user permissions.
8. Price changes not documented.
9. No branch transfer controls.
10. Relying only on year-end counts.

## Quick fixes you can start this week

- Assign one receiving owner per shift.
- Introduce weekly cycle counts for top SKUs.
- Enable low-stock and exception alerts.
- Review variance reports every Monday.

## Final takeaway

Most inventory problems are process problems. Small, consistent controls can produce big improvements in stock reliability and profit.
