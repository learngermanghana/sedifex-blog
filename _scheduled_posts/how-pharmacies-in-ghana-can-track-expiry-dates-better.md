Expiry losses reduce profit and can create compliance risk for pharmacies.

## Why pharmacies lose money on expiry

- No batch-level stock recording.
- Shelves organized by convenience, not expiry date.
- Late reorder and over-purchasing.
- Poor visibility across branches.

## Better expiry management system

1. Record **batch number + expiry date** at receiving.
2. Enforce **FEFO** (First-Expire, First-Out) during dispensing.
3. Set automatic alerts for products expiring in 90/60/30 days.
4. Run weekly near-expiry reports.
5. Transfer near-expiry stock to higher-demand branches.

## KPI targets to monitor

- Expiry loss as % of purchases.
- Near-expiry units on hand.
- Days to expiry by category.
- Batch accuracy score from audits.

## Final takeaway

With batch-level inventory workflows and disciplined FEFO routines, pharmacies in Ghana can dramatically reduce expiry losses and protect patient trust.
