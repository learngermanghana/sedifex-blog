Manual stock books look cheap, but the hidden costs are often high.

## Manual tracking cost buckets

- Time spent on daily reconciliations.
- Sales lost from stockouts and pricing errors.
- Expiry and damage not detected early.
- Leakage from untracked adjustments.

## Software cost buckets

- Subscription or setup fees.
- Staff onboarding and process changes.
- Device costs (POS terminals, scanners).

## Example monthly comparison (SME with 800 SKUs)

- Manual process losses + inefficiency: **GHS 4,000–12,000**.
- Software + operations cost: **GHS 800–3,500**.

Actual numbers vary, but many businesses recover software cost through lower waste and higher stock accuracy.

## How to estimate your own ROI

1. Calculate monthly stock loss and stockout-related lost sales.
2. Add owner/manager hours spent fixing stock errors.
3. Compare this total against software cost.
4. Track outcomes for 90 days after implementation.

## Final takeaway

In most cases, manual tracking is only cheaper on paper. Software usually wins on total cost once losses, time, and growth needs are included.
