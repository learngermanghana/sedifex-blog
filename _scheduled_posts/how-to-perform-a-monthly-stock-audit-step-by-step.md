Monthly stock audits help detect leakage, process gaps, and system errors before they grow.

## Step-by-step monthly audit process

1. Freeze stock movements for selected sections.
2. Print or export count sheets by location.
3. Assign independent counters and supervisors.
4. Count physically, then recount high-variance items.
5. Compare physical counts to system quantities.
6. Categorize variance causes (damage, theft, posting delay, etc.).
7. Approve adjustments with manager sign-off.
8. Document lessons and corrective actions.

## Audit best practices

- Audit high-value and fast-moving items first.
- Use blind counts where possible.
- Keep evidence photos for damaged stock.
- Track recurring variances by staff/process.

## Final takeaway

A monthly stock audit is not just counting; it is a management control system. Consistency is what improves accuracy over time.
