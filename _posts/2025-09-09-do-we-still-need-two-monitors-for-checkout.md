## The Traditional Way

For years, many retail shops have relied on two monitors at checkout.  
One screen faces the seller to manage products and payments, while the second screen faces the customer to show items, prices, and totals in real time.

This setup became the standard because it builds trust and transparency. Customers can see exactly what is being added and how the total is calculated.

However, traditional dual-monitor systems come with challenges. Extra screens cost money, require cables and desk space, and are often impractical for small shops, kiosks, mobile sellers, and pop-up businesses.
